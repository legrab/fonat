# Reference Projects

## Escape from the Mushroom Yard / Szökés a gombakertből

This small, intentionally incomplete cross-subject fixture demonstrates Fonat's optional Project capability without coupling it to ordinary lesson planning.

The original story follows four distinct forest animals trying to leave a mushroom yard in the middle of a forest:

- Makk, a squirrel who notices repeating structures;
- Boróka, a badger who understands binary signs;
- Csipke, a hedgehog who divides supplies fairly with fractions;
- Füles, a rabbit who connects clues and keeps the group moving.

The package contains three compact challenge paths:

1. decode a binary gate;
2. continue and compare a structural pattern;
3. divide mushroom baskets using fractions.

It also contains linked Concepts, Exercises, Activity Templates, a story Resource, and explicit contributor opportunities for future high-school computer-science work.

Bebras is used only as pedagogical inspiration for concise, accessible, story-framed computational-thinking problems. No Bebras task, character, wording, or artwork is copied or closely adapted.
